1. introduction to tools

If you were to ask 100 programmers about their workflows you would most certainly get 100 different answers, even among some of the more “normie” engineers who stick by their default colorschemes. I argue that even looking at them as “normies” is wrong. Observed closely enough there will be no one workflow shared. By a workflow I would mean a combination of all the different choices a programmer has made in relation to their tooling, in this way their workflow will be a direct product of their chosen tools. Taking this a step further I would say (and reason for) that this workflow then defines us as programmers and that we therefore should be very aware of the choices in tooling we make.

In this article I will:

  1. Try to encurage more programmers to care about their choice of tools.
  2. Introduce a way to observe your own workflow or stack.
  3. Forget to use I instead of i. . . (probably)
  4. Define some of the reasons programmers care about their tools currently.
  5. Define some of the other reasons programmers should care more!
